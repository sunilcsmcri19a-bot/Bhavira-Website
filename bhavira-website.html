What we do

A cheaper electrode for electrowinning — the process that wins metal from liquid.

Most metal recovery runs on expensive metal cathodes that pit and corrode in harsh, impurity-laden solutions. Our Plastic Chip Electrode is a graphite-polymer composite: simple to make, chemically tough, and built to carry high current. It does the same job for a fraction of the electrode cost.

The problem today

Conventional cathodes are costly, pit and corrode in acid, and need labour-intensive stripping to harvest the metal.

That means replacement cost, downtime, and lost efficiency — every cycle.

Our electrode

A solution-cast composite that resists corrosion, sustains high current density, and releases its deposit by gravity.

Lower cost, longer life, and metal recovered from streams others treat as waste.

For the mining industry

Turning tailings from liability into resource.

Every tailings dam and waste stream still holds metal — often critical minerals the world now urgently needs. Conventional recovery is too costly to justify on dilute, dirty streams. A low-cost, corrosion-resistant electrode changes that economics.

The gap

Valuable and critical metals sit locked in tailings and effluents because existing electrodes are too expensive and corrode too fast to recover them economically.

What we offer

A cheaper, durable cathode that makes recovery from low-grade and secondary streams viable — extra revenue and reduced waste from assets you already hold.

Why now

Critical-mineral demand and circular-economy pressure are reshaping mining. Recovering metal from waste is moving from cost centre to opportunity.
